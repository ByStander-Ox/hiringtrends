April 2014 Hacker News Hiring Trends

Thursday, April 3, 2014

JavaScript takes the top spot for the second straight month and as it has for most of the past year. The top 5 as a whole remains unchanged in April: JavaScript, Python, iOS, Android and Ruby. Out of the top 10, MySQL is the new entrant, holding off many competitors in the database world.

As far as emerging technology goes, node.js is making a strong run and appears likely to continue its growth. It notched its third straight top 10, despite being somewhat undercounted (there are 3 major spelling variations, and I currently only capture 2 of them). Other technologies to keep an eye on are Docker, Ansible and Kafka which make their debut in the "Rising" category showing significant growth in the last year.

Speaking of databases, mongodb is showing a strong 2nd place in the matchup of mysql, mongodb and postgresql, the three major data persistence technologies in the discussions.

Finally, we've all heard the hype about Big Data and Data Science,and its clear that is making its way into these job postings. In looking at the combination of Hadoop, Data Science, Statistics and Big Data, all show sustained numbers.

About

These charts show what's popular and trending in programming languagues, frameworks and technologies used to develop modern software applications as indicated by Hacker News activity. Using the HN Search API and a dictionary of software terms, all of the the comments (job postings) in the "whoishiring" submissions from April 2011 to March 2014 were processed to count the number of times each software term was mentioned.
