September 2015 Hacker News Hiring Trends

Tuesday, September 8, 2015

For September, Python was the winner, continuing its run of first place finishes this year. But, in a new development, Down goes JavaScript! For the first time since I can remember, JavaScript did not finish in the top 2 spots, falling down to #4. It appears the mobile platforms may be ascending to the top of the programming jobs pyramid with iOS at #2 and Android at #3.

With 794 postings (as of this writing), July set a new all-time record for "Who is hiring?", just surpassing April's previous mark of 785. If only every first of the month fell on a business day, there'd be even more job opportunities posted.

May 2015 Hacker News Hiring Trends

Thursday, May 21, 2015

The most interesting trend from May is the continuing, quick rise of the React JavaScript library. While AngularJS was a pioneer of meteoric rises, React's may end up being a faster climb to the top of the JavaScript library war. It just zoomed past Backbone.js into 2nd place after just about a year since it was first mentiond in the hiring posts.

About

These charts show what's popular and trending in programming languagues, frameworks and technologies used to develop modern software applications as indicated by Hacker News activity. Using the HN Search API and a dictionary of software terms, all of the the comments (job postings) in the "whoishiring" submissions from April 2011 to March 2014 were processed to count the number of times each software term was mentioned.
