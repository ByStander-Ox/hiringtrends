November 2014 Hacker News Hiring Trends

Thursday, November 13, 2014

JavaScript racks up its third win in a row with another strong showing, leaving Python to come in second again. Rails, Ruby and iOS round out the top 5. Elsewhere in the top 10, a little shakeup had node.js and AWS (amazon web services, i.e. "the cloud") coming up and Java taking a big tumble out of the Top 10.

October 2014 Hacker News Hiring Trends

Friday, October 3, 2014

JavaScript, JavaScript, JavaScript. For the sixth month in 2014, JavaScript takes the top spot. Python is still hanging around in its usual #2 spot. In terms of big movers this month, Rails picked up a big gain to take #3 which, I believe is the highest its ever risen. Also, Java made a big jump to #4 after falling out of the top 10 last month.

September 2014 Hacker News Hiring Trends

Saturday, September 13, 2014

Labor Day, of all days, was the day for September's whoishiring thread. Because of the holiday, there there was a big drop from August's record number of postings. Along with the drop in total postings, there was no big shakeup at the top, with most of the top 10 or 20 holding close to their same ranking.

There were some big movers in the Rising chart, with Docker and Ansible continuing their assault in the respective markets.

May 2014 Hacker News Hiring Trends

Saturday, May 3, 2014

Python is the winner, its third win in the last six months as it and JavaScript continue to battle it out. The top 5 remains very similar, with Android, iOS and Ruby maintaining a very consistent presence. node.js and Rails reign supreme for server-side web development, with node now a fixture in the top 10 while AngularJS has established itself firmly in the top 10 after a meteoric rise this past 12-18 months.

About

These charts show what's popular and trending in programming languagues, frameworks and technologies used to develop modern software applications as indicated by Hacker News activity. Using the HN Search API and a dictionary of software terms, all of the the comments (job postings) in the "whoishiring" submissions from April 2011 to March 2014 were processed to count the number of times each software term was mentioned.
